🎓 Eğitim İçeriği Örnek (English Only)

### 🫧 Introduction to Scuba Diving

Scuba diving is an underwater activity that allows you to explore the ocean using specialized equipment. It requires knowledge of pressure physics, gas management, and decompression theory.

### ⬇️ Pressure and Depth

Water pressure increases by 1 atmosphere every 10 meters (33 feet). At 30m, the pressure is 4 atm.

### 🫁 Air Consumption Rate (SAC)

Your Surface Air Consumption (SAC) is how much air you consume per minute at the surface:

**Formula (Metric):**

SAC Rate (L/min) = (Tank Pressure Used × Tank Volume) / (Dive Time × Ambient Pressure)

### 🕒 No Decompression Limits (NDL)

Each depth has a maximum time before decompression is needed. For example:

- 18m (60ft): 56 minutes
- 30m (100ft): 20 minutes

Use dive tables or dive computers to plan accordingly.

### ⬆️ Surface Intervals

Surface intervals allow your body to release nitrogen. More time = more flexibility on next dive.

### 🛟 Safety Stops

Always perform a 3-minute stop at 5m (15ft) after any dive deeper than 10m.
