Why You Need a Dive Computer

A dive computer is one of the most important pieces of equipment in modern scuba diving. It tracks your depth, bottom time, nitrogen loading, and calculates safe ascent profiles in real-time. Whether you're a recreational diver exploring coral reefs or a technical diver planning complex multi-gas dives, choosing the right dive computer is crucial for both safety and enjoyment.

In this comprehensive guide, we've tested and reviewed the best dive computers available in 2025. Our selections are based on reliability, features, user-friendliness, and value for money. How to Choose the Right Dive Computer

1. Determine Your Diving Level

  • Beginner (Open Water): Mares Puck Pro+, Suunto D5
  • Intermediate (Advanced/Rescue): Suunto D5, Garmin Descent
  • Technical Diver: Shearwater Perdix AI

2. Consider Your Budget

  • Under $300: Mares Puck Pro+
  • $500-$800: Suunto D5, Oceanic Pro Plus 4
  • $1000+: Garmin Descent Mk2i, Shearwater Perdix AI

3. Air Integration

Do you want wireless tank pressure monitoring? This adds $200-400 to your budget but provides real-time gas consumption data and time remaining calculations.
Recommended: Shearwater Perdix AI, Garmin Descent Mk2i

4. Form Factor

  • Wrist-mounted: Most popular, easy to read during dive
  • Console: Traditional, all instruments in one place
  • Watch-style: Dual-use as everyday watch
